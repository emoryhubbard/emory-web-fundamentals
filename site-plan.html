Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this site is to provide our customers with a place where they can experience what Dry Oar whitewater rafting is like. This will encourage them to choose our company for their next family adventure. This site is meant to share various forms of content to allow these families to get excited about us and their next whitewater rafting adventure. It is common for our visitors to take many pictures during their experience, and this site is intended to help future visitors see what it is like through such images. This is a place where they share their experience as well through images, video, and descriptions.

Audience

Our target audience is a wide range of families. Many families have children that they seek to create memorable experiences with and this site will help guide them to making those memories with us. Others are older couples wishing to continue making memorable experiences with one another. Any family, including husband and wife alone, that seeks to have more memorable family experiences can help have their needs met with our site. They will typically access our site from a mobile device due to searches on google maps. They will have decent disposable income and have an interest in the outdoors, adventure, or sports that involve the outdoors.

Typography

Heading Font: Anybody

One of the goals for choosing this font was to find something that had a personality with a connotation of adventure. I chose this font because I had more flexibility for the heading and this one stood out from the others in being easy to read and having a level of fun to it without being over-the-top.

Paragraph Font: Verdana

This is a classic web-safe font that suited the needs of the site to have a good readable body. I chose it instead of the others because it had more of a casual personality, suitable for a website for families looking for a fun experience.

Normal paragraph example

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
